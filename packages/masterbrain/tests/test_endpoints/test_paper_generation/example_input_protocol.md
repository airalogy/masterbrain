# Synthesis of Homochiral Gold Nanopropeller Particles

## Overview

This experiment synthesizes homochiral gold nanopropeller particles using a seed-mediated, multi-step growth approach, essential for applications in sensing, biomedicine, and catalysis.

## Experiment Procedures

### 1. Reagent Preparation

| Reagent        | Weight (mg) | Solvent Volume (mL) |
| -------------- | ----------- | ------------------- |
| HAuCl4 (50 mM) | 250 mg      | 5 mL                |
| Citrate (5 mM) | 29.4 mg     | 10 mL               |
| NaBH4 (25 mM)  | 95 mg       | 10 mL               |
| CTAC (50 mM)   | 320 mg      | 20 mL               |

* **Note:** Dissolve NaBH4 in cold water (3-8°C) to avoid air/moisture sensitivity.

### 2. Seed Solution Synthesis

1. Combine 10 mL deionized water, 160 mg CTAC, and 14.7 mg sodium citrate.
2. Add 50 μL HAuCl4 (50 mM) and stir until clear pale yellow.
3. Add 0.25 mL NaBH4 (25 mM); color changes to brown.
4. Heat in an 80°C oil bath for 90 minutes, resulting in a red solution. Final Au concentration is 0.25 mM.

### 3. Gold Decahedra Synthesis

1. In a 30°C water bath, dissolve BDAC (100 mL, 100 mM), then add HAuCl4 (1 mL, 50 mM) and ascorbic acid (0.75 mL, 100 mM).
2. Add 68 μL seed solution to the mixture.
3. The solution turns red after 30 minutes. Wash with deionized water and concentrate 10-fold, storing in 1 mM CTAB with a final concentration of 1.1×10¹⁵ particles/L.

### 4. Homochiral Gold Nanopropeller Synthesis

| Reagent                    | Weight (mg) | Solvent Volume (mL) |
| -------------------------- | ----------- | ------------------- |
| HAuCl4 (10 mM)             | 50 mg       | 5 mL                |
| CTAB (0.1 M)               | 172 mg      | 20 mL               |
| Ascorbic acid (AA) (0.1 M) | 30 mg       | 10 mL               |
| GSH (1.5 mg/10 mL)         | 0.15 mg     | 10 mL               |

1. Combine 10 mL H2O, 0.2 mL HAuCl4 (10 mM), and 0.8 mL CTAB (0.1 M) in a 20 mL vial.
2. Add 0.475 mL AA (0.1 M) and shake; solution turns from yellow to colorless.
3. Add 1 μL of gold decahedra solution.
4. Let the reaction proceed at 30°C for 2 hours; color changes from colorless to blue-purple to brown.

### Experimental Results and Notes

* **Seed Solution Result:** Clear pale yellow color after NaBH4 addition, changing to red after heating.
* **Gold Decahedra Result:** Red-colored solution, particles washed and concentrated 10-fold.
* **Gold Nanopropeller Result:** Blue-purple solution after 2 hours of reaction at 30°C, final product is a brownish color indicating successful synthesis.
